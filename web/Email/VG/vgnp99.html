The 1999 Vermont Group No-Press Tournament

AWARDS
Tournament Winner: Malcolm Dow!
The WAFFLE (Utterly Useless Award For Fewest Loses/Eliminations): Christian Hagenah, David Cox and Malcolm Dow all win a share of the Eggo, with only two losses in seven games.
The Bronze Bagel, given as consolation to those unfortunate souls who mustered nary a point in the competiton: Brian Keislich, Dale Grantham, Gordon Riley, Lynn Mercer, Paul Quintin, Peter Taylor, Rob Addison, Ron Artigues, Ronald Lokers, Stephane Derdi, and William Romack.
Synchronicity Award, given to the player whose personal result most closely matched the typical result in this tournament, as indicated by the "Average Power Strengths" listed a few page-downs below. To determine your score, multiply the average score for Austria (which will be some negative number for the powers that did poorly in this tournament and will be some positive number for the powers that did well in this tournament) with your result (a loss is -1, a win is 6, and a draw is 7/N-1). Do this for each power, then sum the results. The largest positive total wins. This year's leader: Malcolm Dow, with +6.93. He did well with the positive powers this year (won with France and Turkey), but lost with the weaker powers (Italy and Russia).
Anti-Synchronicity Award, given to the player whose personal result most severely contradicted the typical result in this tournament. The process is the same as the Synchronicity award, except that the largest *negative* total wins. This year's leader: Christian Hagenah, with -2.17. He managed three-way draws with Italy and Russia, but lost as France and Turkey -- go figure!
Chumbawamba Award, given to the player who "gets knocked down, but he gets up again!" and forges the best comeback in a particular game: Eric Austin, in 9902 as Turkey. In 1912, he was down to four SC's (Con, Smy, Bul, and Gre), while EFIR were at 12-8-6-4. But he gained an average of one SC per year until eventually winning the game in 1928. Honorable mention to the weakest position to ever make it into a draw: Ken Lofgren's 2-SC Austria in year 1911 of game 9932, up against an IGFR at 12-11-8-1 -- yet he still managed a 3WD.
The "Extinguished Service Medal" for the quickest elimination: Sven Vasseur, 1902, in game 9915 as Austria (who else?).
Anti-Kevorkian Honor for latest elimination: Jeff Serandos, 1929, in game 9924 as Italy.
Roman Empire Award, for the player who most manages to build up the biggest empire, and still have it crumble back to nothing. Measured as the highest SC count at any point for any eliminated power in any game (players who survive in a solo don't count). David Proudfoot in 9917, whose 11-SC France in 1911 was stuck between a 12-SC Turkey and an 11-SC England, who squeezed him out over the next twelve painful years before declaring a 2WD.
The Outer Preference Award, for the player who does best with outer powers compared to inner powers. Defined as the player with highest value for (Average gamescore with EFRT) - (Average gamescore with AGI): Randy Christie, whose EFRT powers went loss-solo-solo-loss, but whose AGI powers went loss-loss-loss.
The Inner Preference Award, for the player who does best with inner powers compared to outer powers. Defined as the player with highest value for (Average gamescore with AGI) - (Average gamescore with EFRT): David Proudfoot, whose AGI powers went loss-solo-loss, but picked up four losses with EFRT.
The Western Preference Award, for the player who does best with Western powers compared to Eastern powers. Defined as the player with highest value for (Average gamescore with EFG) - (Average gamescore with AIRT): Gytis Karciauskas, whose EFG went 3WD-solo-solo, but whose AIRT went L-L-L-L.
The Eastern Preference Award, for the player who does best with Eastern powers compared to Western powers. Defined as the player with highest value for (Average gamescore with AIRT) - (Average gamescore with EFG): Pascal Hitzler, whose AIRT went solo-loss-3WD-loss, but whose EFG went L-L-L.
The Wicked Witches Preference Award, for the player who does best with witches compared to the other powers. Defined as the player with highest value for (Average gamescore with ET) - (Average gamescore with AFGIR): Jason Whitby, who scored solos with England and Turkey, but "only" went solo-L-L-L-L with AFGIR.
The Consistancy award, for the player who scores his points slowly and steadily. Defined as the player with a positive final score whose seven scores have the lowest standard deviation: Dave Cox, whose 5 3WD and 2 losses had a SD of 1.09 (I think!)
The Death or Glory award, for the player who either does very well or very badly. Defined as the player who's seven scores have the highest standard deviation: Jason Whitby, with three solos and four losses, had an SD of 3.46.

where Sum[SC^2] is the sum of the squares of all the powers in the game.

and the score for a win is 6 points. Eliminated or surviving powers lose 1 point.
