The list itself is right HERE. It's kinda big (1.71 Mb). Five numbers are given for each player: A unique ID number, the Judge Diplomacy Player Rating, JDPR games played, Diplomacy Resignation Record games played, and resignations from DRR games. The games played totals overlap for the most part, except that (a) games older than three years old can't be counted in the DRR and (b) games with certain Irregular flag settings aren't counted in the JDPR. Many players have aliases -- multiple name/email combinations that are used by the same person -- I highly encourage everyone to check this list and help me combine multiple email addresses under single entries. If you see two entries for the same person, email me, Doug Massey, with the information.

Visit the Diplomatic Pouch for more information on the Judge Diplomacy Player Ratings.

I encourage the use of this list as a tool to help GM's determine which players are likely to be reliable (that is, likely to play his or her power until the completion of the game or that power's elimination). The goal is for all public games on the Diplomacy Email Judges to be entered automatically into the database that produces this list; so far, only some judges report game finishes automatically (USIN, USVG, USEF, DEAC, USCA, FROG, USBR, CAMA, USCH, and USTR), while other judges (USPA, USWI, CAWA, and SEPO) are polled manually for finished games. This polling is not guaranteed to catch every game and *cannot* catch *any* NoList game.

Here's how Resignations and Completed Games are counted.

Completed Game: Any game where you are the last person to play a power in the game.

Resignation: Any game where you play a power, but are not the last person to play that power. Exceptions: you can't be penalized for RTDip games, NMR games, terminated games, or other various irregular type games (as defined at the JDPR). Also, in the case of a temporary replacement (defined as player A being replaced by a series of one or more players before coming back to play that power again), the original power is only counted once and none of the replacement players count at all. So if the summary looked like this: 

  Germany:       Player A
   from F1902M:  Player B
   from F1904M:  Player C
   from F1906M:  Player A
   from F1908M:  Player D
  Italy:         Player E
   from F1903M:  Player F
   from F1905M:  Player G
   from F1907M:  Player F
  Russia:        Player H
   from S1904M:  Player I
   from F1909M:  Player J

then the players records would be affected as such:

Players A, E, H, and I each pick up one game and one resignation (G++, R++)
Players D, F, and J each pick up one completed game (G++)
Players B, C, and G don't change at all.

A private enclave called The Vermont Group has been formed as an organization of games for dedicated Diplomacy Players. These games are available only to members of the Group. To join, you must have a ratio of (G-R)/(R+1) greater than 4.0, where G = games played and R = resignations, as indicated in this Resignation Record. If you find your name on the list linked below and your ratio is higher than 4.0, then you are eligible to join the Vermont Group by emailing me or by requesting to be included in a Vermont Group game at the web site.
