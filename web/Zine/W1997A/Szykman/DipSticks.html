We're back with a couple more word puzzles -- this time two different "acrostic" grids for you to fit the 34 supply centers into. But first, some unfinished business....

For those of you who took a shot at the word search puzzle in the previous issue of the Zine, you can find the solution here. Many thanks to Dann Fuller (dannimal@umich.edu) for taking the time to scan it in and send it to us.

Now, here's another supply center word puzzle for your enjoyment. The goal is to fill in the squares below with the names of all 34 supply centers (leave off the period in St. Petersburg). No clues, no numbers, just a lot of empty blanks. Simply print this page and fit the names of the 34 supply centers into the grid. It's not as easy as it sounds, though! There's just one possible solution, but only by a thoroughly enjoyable application of grey matter can you know which supply center goes into which spot!
