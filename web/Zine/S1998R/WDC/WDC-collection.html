World DipCon VIII

A Collection of Reports and Photographs

A number of people from The Diplomatic Pouch and its readership were on hand at the joint DipCon XXXI/World DipCon VIII, held at DixieCon in Chapel Hill, North Carolina. Now you can experience the event vicariously through their words.

  • In Impressions From DipCon, by Buz Eddy, you can see what one of the most respected veterans has to say about the experience.
  • To contrast with that, check out what a complete newbie took away from the event. For this, you need nothing more than Jon Mlinar's I Love This Game (the title kind of ruins any suspense you might have had, doesn't it?)
  • The inimitable Larry Peery has typed up his own take, with his Who Says You Can't Go Home Again? This musical article is located at Larry's personal Website (not here at The Pouch).
  • Also on Larry's personal pages, you can find (and should definitely check out) his World DipCon Shrine! There you can find out all you need to know about the history of our World Championship Tournament. See memorabilia and photos from WDC's past!
  • How about a few words from our new World Champion, Chris Martin? Okay. In Reflections on the Merits of Becoming World Champion, Chris forwards us a bunch of mail he has received since his achievement, so that we can all suffer through his good fortune with him.
  • Not enough from Mr. Martin? Okay. He also chimes in with Be The Little Guy, telling us all exactly how he went about becoming the World Champion. It's now a tried and tested formula, so it'll probably never work again.
  • In What I Learned at World DipCon, David Willis relates...well, he relates what he learned at World DipCon. (What did you expect?)
  • Jamie McQuinn supplies his own recollections of Chapel Hill in his Notes From World DipCon 8. See what The Pouch's Postal Section leader has to say about this latest annual gathering.
  • Scott Morris, showing the stuff true Diplomacy players are made of, played while ill, but had more than enough fun to write all about it in Fear and Loathing at World DipCon.
  • Simon Szykman provides Yet Another World DipCon Report. Read how Simon, a multiple award winner (!), viewed the stabfest.
  • Last but certainly not least, be sure to check out the Photographs from the Convention, where you can either see what you missed, or re-live the good time you had! The photos were either contributed by David Norman (thanks, David!) or stolen with a bald face from Larry Peery's article (thanks, Larry!)
