Review

DSI fills a different role than the Hall of Fame. HoF is an all-inclusive system which produces cumulative lifetime achievement ratings that span a wide range of variants and Diplomacy-like games. Plus it encompasses both starting and replacement players.

DSI on the other hand produces an skill rating - an average per game. DSI's approach is minimalist. It intentionally rates only the performance of starting players (_not_ replacement players) and only for standard Diplomacy games. DSI also weights results based on the relative difficulty of the seven countries. Plus DSI has a dampener to reduce the advantage that folks with just a few game completions would otherwise have.

DSI's basic point system is:
Win --------- 7 points
N way draw -- 7/N points
Survival ---- 1/2 point
Elimination - 1/4 point
Replaced ---- 0 points

Further detail on DSI can be found in the introductory article, which is at ftp.ugcs.caltech.edu/pub/diplomacy/DSI/article.1.

Country Stats

This section applies DSI to the seven powers. This gives the relative strengths of the powers which are later used to weight the player statistics. In the following tables you'll see lots of lines like: 

   1.13 Germany W:01 2:00 3:05 4:00 5:01 6:00 7:00 S:004 E:011 G:22

That would tell you that Germany had a DSI of 1.13. It would tell you also that Germany had 1 win, 5 three-ways, 1 five-ways, 4 survivals, and 11 eliminations for a total of 22 games.
