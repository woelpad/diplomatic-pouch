Terrain Build Costs
Plains, Towns: 1 (basic build cost)
Mountain: +2 per half-hex
River: +2 per hex-side

Actual Build Costs
1: Plains - Plains
3: Plains - River - Plains; Plains - Mountain
5: Mountain - Mountain; Plains - River - Mountain

Start Positions
3 Players: Melbourne (52)
4-6 Players: Melbourne (52), Geelong, Ballarat, Bendigo.
                  More than one player may start at each location.

Specials
1. NSW towns that were connected to the Victorian railnet: Any of the 8 towns labelled NSW.
2. Connections to NSW State Railways and Sydney: Any hex containing NSW State Rail Track (inc. Albury, Oaklands & Tocumwal).
3. Connections to SA State Railways and Adelaide: Any hex containing SA State Rail Track (inc. R3).
4. Port: Portland, Geelong, Echuca, Melbourne, Western Port (In postal games, Echuca is not available in rounds 10-12).
5. 1-3 Gold: St Arnaud, Bendigo, Maryborough, Castlemaine, Ballarat, Bright, or any of the 9 hexes labelled 'G'.
5. 4-6 Coal: Any of the 4 hexes labelled 'C'.
6. 1-3 Timber: Obost or any of the 8 hexes labelled 'T'.
6. 4-6 Wheat: Any of the 6 hexes labelled 'W'.

Special Rules

  • Players may build and race through NSW and SA.
  • Treat NSW and SA State Rail Track like another player's for junction & paralleling fees.
  • NSW & SA State Rail TRack may be raced along. Hire cost is $1/hex.
  • All build and racing payments to NSW and SA are paid to the bank.
  • There is a 1 die-pip penalty when changing between a player's track and NSW or SA State Rail Track (and vice versa due to gauge differences.
  • All players are considered to be the same guage.
  • Half cities pay $3 each when connected.
  • Third cities pay $2 each when connected.
  • Towns in NSW do not pay connection bonuses.
  • Melbourne metropolitan area consists of 3 seperately numbered city hexes. Players do not pay parallel/junction fees within the metro area.
  • Ringwood and Dandenong do not pay connection bonuses.
  • Minumum race length is 4.
