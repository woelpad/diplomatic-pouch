The Abyssinian Prince

Published by Jim Burgess

The Diplomacy A-Z has a long and storied history. Begun by Mark Berch, updated and revised in the early 90's by Mark Nelson, Harold Reynolds, and Jim Burgess, and now put on the Web in a gloriously colorful new version by Harold Reynolds, no postal Diplomacy person should be without this link. Check out the Diplomacy A-Z right now!!!

Below are a selection of the most recent issues of my postal/E-Mail/internet Diplomacy gaming szine THE ABYSSINIAN PRINCE, named after Rasselas, the great Johnsonian Abyssinian Prince. Also see the revival of Diplomacy World!!! THE NEW ISSUE IS OUT!!! Tim Haffey is the new production lead editor for Diplomacy World to assist me and Stephen Agar, if you want to contribute to the NEXT issue, talk to me now at Jim Burgess!!!

Vote NOW for the 2004 Diplomacy Hobby Awards!!! The Ballot is in PDF Adobe format this year, let me know if that presents you any problems. The 2003 Hobby Awards Ballot and the detailed announcement of the 2002 awards with web links are available.

The 2003 Don Miller Award for Meritorious Service went to Chad Schroeder for the Redscape site.

The 2003 Rod Walker Award for Literature went to Paul Windsor for "Persuasive Illogic".

The 2003 John Koning Award for Player Performance went to Yann Clouet for leading the Diplomatic Pouch Tournament Ratings.

The 2003 Fred Hyatt Award for GM Performance went to Christine Stoy especially for her GM work on Redscape.

See the full Hobby Awards ballots from 2001 with web links for winners and for the runners-up in an HTML version or an Adobe Acrobat version. Send comments to Jim Burgess!!!

The most recent version of the House Rules was published in #211: House Rules.

You can connect to David Partridge's subszine, Tinamou, as it also appears below with some of the issues in which it appeared in the postal version. And Michael Lowrey has a subszine, Houdini Blues, that also is accessible here. Plus, starting with Issue #4, Deny Everything, the subszine by Don Williams, is here in Adobe pdf format, now in html format as well but unfortunately defunct.

Jim Meinel's 1992 Encyclopedia of Postal Diplomacy Zines is now available on the Web in Adobe pdf format too. Comments may be made to Jim Meinel directly, although he does not intend to update it at the present time.
