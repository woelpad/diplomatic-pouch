The Abyssinian Prince

Published by Jim Burgess

The Diplomacy A-Z has a long and storied history. Begun by Mark Berch, updated and revised in the early 90's by Mark Nelson, Harold Reynolds, and Jim Burgess, and now put on the Web in a gloriously colorful new version by Harold Reynolds, no postal Diplomacy person should be without this link. Check out the Diplomacy A-Z right now!!! Mark, Harold and I (with some assistance from Bruce Linsey) have recently updated a number of entries.

Below are a selection of the most recent issues of my postal/E-Mail/internet Diplomacy gaming szine THE ABYSSINIAN PRINCE, named after Rasselas, the great Johnsonian Abyssinian Prince. Diplomacy World is fully revived!!! See some old issues, as well as Doug Kent's new subszine ETERNAL SUNSHINE. If you want to contribute to the NEXT issue of DIPLOMACY WORLD, talk to me now at Jim Burgess!!!

The results for the 2004 Diplomacy Hobby Awards are right here!!! The Ballot is in PDF Adobe format this year. There was some difficulty accessing the Mark Berch Rod Walker Award nominated article, click on the link to read it. The 2003 Hobby Awards Ballot and the detailed announcement of the 2002 awards with web links are available.

The 2004 Don Miller Award for Meritorious Service goes to Christian Shelton for the DipBounced website.

The 2004 Rod Walker Award for Literature goes to Manus Hand for "Solution to the Curious Case of the False Start".

The 2004 John Koning Award for Player Performance goes to Yann Clouet who won the 2004 World DipCon.

The 2004 Fred Hyatt Award for GM Performance goes to Cal White especially for his GM work on DipBOUNCED.

A 2004 Kathy Byrne Caruso Award for Lifetime Achievement goes posthumously to Don Turnbull for founding the UK Postal Hobby.

A 2004 Kathy Byrne Caruso Award for Lifetime Achievement goes to Buz Eddy for his work on the North American Diplomacy Federation.

See the full Hobby Awards ballots from 2001 with web links for winners and for the runners-up in an HTML version or an Adobe Acrobat version. Send comments to Jim Burgess!!!

The most recent version of the House Rules was published in #211: House Rules.

You can connect to David Partridge's subszine, Tinamou, as it also appears below with some of the issues in which it appeared in the postal version. And Michael Lowrey has a subszine, Houdini Blues, but only extreme back issues are accessible here. Beginning with Issue #4, Deny Everything, the subszine by Don Williams, is here in Adobe pdf format, now in html format as well but unfortunately defunct.

Jim Meinel's 1992 Encyclopedia of Postal Diplomacy Zines is now available on the Web in Adobe pdf format too. Comments may be made to Jim Meinel directly, although he does not intend to update it at the present time.
